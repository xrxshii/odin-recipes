Semmelknödel


Semmelknödel is a beloved Austrian dish that most
people make with old hard kaiser rolls.
You can enjoy it either as a main dish with a
sauce or as a side dish with meat dishes.



Ingredients

  • 500g bread cubes
  • 500 ml milk
  • 2 egg
  • 1 onion
  • 3 Tbsp parsley
  • 1 pinch of nutmeg
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 pinch of pepper
  • 3 Tbsp of flour



Needed kitchen-utensils

  • A Bowl
  • A cooking pot
  • A pan
  • A slotted Spoon



Step by step

  1. Warm up the milk and pour it over the
    bread cubes and let it rest a few minutes

  2. Now knead the salt, pepper, nutmeg, the finely chopped parsley and the eggs
    into the bread (best to do with wet hands)

  3. Finely chop the onion and slightly sauté it in a pan with a bit of butter,
    (dont let it get brown) then knead it real good into the dough

  4. Now you can shape the dough into round balls then
    roll it on the flour that you have left

  5. Let the bread dumplings sit in a large pot of slightly boiling salted water for 15 - 20 minutes
    (depending on size) until they rise to the top. Then remove the bread dumplings from the water with a slotted spoon.



Tips for the recipe

Instead of boiling the dumplings in salt water
you could also boil it in vegetable soup

Shaped as mini-dumplings, they make a nice soup garnish

Bread dumplings are a great use for old bread instead
of throwing it away you could do dumplings!

It is especially tasty with gulash or egg recipies.
